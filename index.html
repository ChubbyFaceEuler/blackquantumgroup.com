Research

What we work on

Our core programme is inference for high-frequency, asynchronously observed data — a problem where classical statistics breaks down and careful theory pays off in live systems.

01

High-frequency covariance estimation

Covariance and dependence estimation under asynchronous sampling and microstructure noise, with rigorous asymptotic guarantees carried through to deployable estimators.

02

Market microstructure

Reconstruction and modelling of limit-order-book dynamics from raw order-by-order data; microstructure-aware features for prediction on futures markets.

03

Machine learning for noisy, non-stationary data

ML methods that respect the statistical structure of financial data — regime change, heavy tails, and the ever-present risk of overfitting.

04

Rigorous strategy evaluation

Walk-forward validation, look-ahead detection, and formally verified research pipelines. We treat evaluation methodology as a research problem in its own right.

Approach

How we do research

Theory first

Estimators come with proofs, not just backtests. If we can't state the assumptions, we don't trust the result.

Adversarial evaluation

Every positive result is treated as a bug until it survives walk-forward testing, leakage audits, and independent replication.

Theory to deployment

Research isn't finished at the paper. We carry methods through simulation, real data, and production infrastructure.

Formal verification

Where the mathematics matters most, we machine-check it — bringing proof assistants into quantitative finance.
